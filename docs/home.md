# Quantum Computing

Welcome to the quantum computing volume of the Big Book of Computing. This online book is designed as a structured path from first principles to modern applications, with an emphasis on clarity, mathematical grounding, and computational practice.

Quantum computing is often introduced through headlines about speedups, cryptography, and futuristic machines. This book takes a different route. It starts with the language of states, operators, circuits, and measurement, then builds toward algorithms, quantum machine learning, simulation, hardware, and error correction. The goal is not just to describe the field, but to make it readable, teachable, and usable.

## What This Book Covers

The material is organized into four broad arcs:

- Foundations of quantum computing, including qubits, linear algebra, states, operators, gates, and circuits.
- Core algorithmic ideas such as oracle algorithms, the quantum Fourier transform, and variational methods.
- Data-driven and application-oriented topics including quantum machine learning, optimization, simulation, chemistry, and finance.
- Hardware realities, noise, and error correction, which define what practical quantum computation can become.

## How To Use This Site

This site is intended to support different reading styles.

- Read the main chapter essays for conceptual development and narrative explanation.
- Use the workbook pages for guided exercises, checkpoints, and active study.
- Use the codebook pages to connect theory with implementation and experimentation.

If you are new to the subject, start with the [Introduction](chapters/introduction.md), then use the [Contents](chapters/contents.md) page to choose a path through the material.

## Who This Is For

This book is written for readers who want more than a popular overview but do not necessarily begin as specialists.

- Students entering quantum information, computation, or applied mathematics.
- Engineers and software developers moving from classical computing to quantum workflows.
- Researchers who want a compact, connected reference across theory, algorithms, tools, and applications.
- Self-learners who prefer a book-like structure over disconnected notes and tutorials.

## Reading Path

If you want a practical progression, use this sequence:

1. Start with Chapters 1 to 3 for the core mathematical and circuit model.
2. Move to Chapters 4 to 6 for the main algorithmic patterns.
3. Use Chapters 7 to 15 to connect theory with software tools and learning-based methods.
4. Finish with Chapters 16 to 20 for advanced applications, hardware, and fault tolerance.

## Book Structure

The current online volume contains 20 chapters, each with supporting workbook and codebook material. The structure is intentionally cumulative: later chapters assume fluency with the conceptual language established earlier.

Use the navigation tabs to move directly into essays, exercises, or implementation notes, depending on how you want to study.
